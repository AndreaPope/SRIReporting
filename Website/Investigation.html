Social Justice: Human Rights Campaign (HRC)

2016-2017 Gains and Losses

According to the Human Rights Commission, major companies in recent years have accelerated their efforts to improve policies affecting LGBTQ employees and their families. The HRC assesses these efforts in their annual Corporate Equality Index (CEI) report. In 2017, more than 100 publicly traded companies posted gains in their HRC scores and a few lost points.

Hover over the bars in the chart below to see the companies and analyst recommendations for whether it is a good time to buy or sell a particular stock.

The recommendations used here are as of November 12, 2017.

Television cable company Charter Communications (CHTR) and aerospace and defense giant Lockheed Martin (LM) posted the most gains. Genuine Parts Company (GPC), which sells and distributes car parts and electronics, lost the most points year over year. Also notably, the big box store Costco (COST) lost points because it does not enforce non-discrimination standards that include sexual orientation and gender identity for its vendors. Costco also lost points because it does not have transgender-inclusive health insurance coverage.

Environment: Greenhouse Gas Emissions

CDP Report on Carbon Emissions

Companies are placed into Performance Bands by the Carbon Disclosure Project (CDP) report used as the basis for these Carbon Emission reports. Performance Bands range from A (the best) to E (the worst). Note, this is not directly related to total metric tons of carbon emissions, but based on the companyâ€™s performance in comparison to their peers within their sector. Specifically, performance points are awarded for evidence considered to contribute to climate change mitigation, adaptation and transparency. Actions considered to be more fundamental to progress on combating climate change are awarded more points. External verification of emissions data is rewarded under the performance scoring as it is considered that this potentially increases the usefulness of the information to decision makers and can act as a pre-qualifier of the data submitted that should be recognized when evaluating performance.

The performance score is also based solely on a company's CDP response. To receive a performance score, a company must achieve a minimum disclosure score of 50. This is to ensure that the judgement is based on sufficient information. Performance scores are converted to percentages in a similar way to the disclosure score percentage is generated. At the end of scoring, the number of points a company has been awarded is divided by the maximum number that could have been awarded. The fraction is then converted to a percentage by multiplying by 100 and rounded to the nearest whole number. (Points awarded / points attainable) x 100 = Disclosure Score For additional information on the scoring mechanism, see CDP.
